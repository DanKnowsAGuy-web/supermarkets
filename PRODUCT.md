# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hotel General Managers, owners, and especially **multi-property portfolio operators**. They control energy spend across one or many properties and carry P&L responsibility for it.

Their context: skeptical and time-poor. They have been burned by energy vendors who over-promised savings and under-delivered. They do not have the time, and do not want, to evaluate energy technology themselves. They are arriving cold, often from an outreach touch, scanning for a reason to disqualify this as more vendor noise.

The job to be done: decide, in a few minutes, whether Energy Plus is worth a single conversation. They are not buying a device. They are deciding whether to risk their time on a partner who claims they can be trusted on energy savings.

## Product Purpose

A conversion-focused landing page for **Energy Plus**, a vendor-neutral energy strategy partnership for hotels. The page sells expertise and rigorous verification, not any single product. Energy Plus is vendor-neutral by design: the offer is conservative, verified savings backed by a measurement protocol, not a gadget.

Success is one action: the operator books a **verified pilot conversation**. Single CTA throughout.

The page must do its persuasion through structure, not volume. It carries an embedded savings calculator so operators model their own numbers, conservative-floor case-study data from real installs with transparent extrapolation to a hotel envelope (math shown, nothing inflated), and a two-door information architecture (below).

## Brand Personality

Plain, declarative, confident, conservative by design. Three words: **rigorous, understated, trustworthy.**

Voice understates rather than overstates, on purpose: we quote the conservative savings *floor*, never the peak. No hype, no buzzwords, no em dashes. The emotional goal is earned trust through restraint, the feeling of dealing with a quiet expert who has nothing to inflate.

Strategic framing the design serves:
- **StoryBrand spine**: the operator is the hero; Energy Plus is the guide with empathy plus authority.
- **Chet Holmes stadium-pitch open**: lead with the macro stake (poor power quality, unmanaged load), not the product.
- **Klaff**: selective, not needy. Not every facility qualifies.
- **Hormozi risk reversal**: verify first, commit after.
- **Jay Abraham preeminence**: act in the operator's interest, even against our own short-term interest.

The core reframe: skepticism is the qualifier, not the objection. We tell operators they are *right* to be skeptical, that verifying energy claims is genuinely hard, and that we will not move forward until we verify their actual savings at their property. The strongest trust signal is that we measure the conservative floor, not peak savings, on purpose.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template**: no gradient-text hero, no identical three-card feature grid, no tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow over every section, no big-number hero-metric template.
- **Greenwash eco-brand**: no leafy green, no earthy "sustainability" trope. This is rigor and finance, not virtue signalling.
- **Cheap energy-vendor**: none of the loud, salesy, over-promising solar/LED-retrofit lead-gen look the audience has been burned by.
- **Crypto/fintech flash**: no neon-on-black, no animated number tickers, no aggressive gradients. Restraint signals competence here.

## Design Principles

1. **Verification is the offer.** Surface the measurement rigor (conservative floor, before-and-after protocol) as the product, not as fine print. Complexity is positioned as proof of our competence, never as homework for the operator.
2. **Two doors, one path.** A simple default surface for delegators (the floor number, the calculator, the guarantee) with an optional "show me the method" depth layer for skeptics (power factor, THD, measurement protocol). The operator is never asked to assess a reading; they are shown that someone who already can, does.
3. **Understate to earn trust.** Every claim lands below what we could say. The conservative floor is a feature, not a hedge.
4. **Selective, not needy.** The page qualifies the reader. It is allowed to say not every facility is a fit. Restraint over persuasion volume.
5. **Skepticism is the qualifier.** Design with the burned, doubtful operator in mind; meet doubt with acknowledgement and proof, never with louder claims.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text holds ≥4.5:1 against its background (the dark navy palette needs careful tuning so slate blue-grey body never drops below the floor; bump toward the off-white ink end where it gets close). Large text ≥3:1. Full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic landmarks. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative. Calculator is operable and labelled for assistive tech.
